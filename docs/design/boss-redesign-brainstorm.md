# BattleCraps — Boss Redesign Brainstorm

> **Context:** Design analysis of all 9 floor bosses. Covers current mechanic assessment, player feedback, redesign ideas, and final recommendations. Companion to `boss-mechanic-technical-design.md`.
>
> **Design goals:** Challenging but fair | Each boss targets a different dominant strategy | Variety of battle experience | Thematic appropriateness

---

## Floor 1 — The Foreman
**Rule:** EXTORTION_FEE — 20% tax on all winning payouts

### Assessment
Works well as an intro boss. The flat percentage tax is immediately legible — players feel it on their very first win. It doesn't block any strategy; it just makes every strategy 20% less efficient. Thematically perfect: a corrupt site supervisor skimming off the top.

**Player feedback:** Fine as-is.

### Verdict
**No changes recommended.** The Foreman is the correct difficulty for F1 and serves as a clean tutorial boss for the extortion mechanic. Don't touch it.

---

## Floor 2 — Sarge
**Rule:** RISING_MIN_BETS — minimum bet increases each turn

### Assessment
The concept is right — rising pressure, mounting urgency — but the execution is too toothless at F2 stakes ($300/$600/$1k targets). Players regularly clear the first marker before the minimum reaches a genuinely punishing level. The Grinder/Doorman additive build generates passive income through NO_RESOLUTION rolls regardless of bet size, so rising minimums don't actually pressure the dominant build. Most players report needing only 1-2 point hits to clear.

**Player feedback:** Too easy. Cleared in 1-2 points.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — Penalty for Non-Compliance**
Missing the minimum doesn't just block the bet — it triggers a bankroll fine (5% of marker target). Players can now *choose* not to meet the minimum, but they pay for it. Creates a genuine decision each roll: absorb the fine or place the bet.

**Option B — Odds Bets Included**
Rising minimums apply to odds bets as well as pass line. Odds are the high-leverage play; forcing minimum odds bets eats bankroll fast and creates real tension about whether to take odds at all. Currently the player can just not take odds to avoid the pressure.

**Option C — Supply Sergeant**
Crew that fire on NO_RESOLUTION (Grinder) must "pay for their action" — each trigger costs a small flat bankroll fee. Turns Grinder's 65-70% fire rate from a pure asset into something with a running cost. Thematically: Sarge taxes inactivity.

**Option D — Success Penalty**
The minimum spikes on each *point hit* in addition to the normal per-roll increase. Players who clear points quickly (exactly who beats Sarge in 1-2 points) get punished by their own efficiency. Forces a tradeoff between clearing markers fast and keeping minimum bets manageable.

### Verdict
**Light change recommended.** Options A + B together. Non-compliance fines give players an agency release valve (pay the fine or meet the minimum), while extending minimums to odds bets creates genuine bankroll pressure that the current version entirely avoids. Neither requires significant backend changes. Supply Sergeant (Option C) is the most interesting idea long-term but higher implementation cost.

## Floor 3 — Mme. Le Prix
**Rule:** DISABLE_CREW — all crew disabled for the floor

### Assessment
The hardest-feeling boss despite the user noting she's not actually that hard. The problem is the experience — "no crew" means no cascade, no interactions, no decisions. The floor becomes vanilla craps with nothing special happening in either direction. Boring is worse than hard. Players aren't engaged, they're just waiting for marker-clear math to resolve. The mechanic also doesn't interact with any strategy because it eliminates all of them simultaneously.

**Player feedback:** Not hard, but boring. No decision-making on the floor.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — The Tariff**
Crew aren't disabled. Instead, every additive generated by a crew member is reduced by 35% (her cut). Multipliers and hype ticks are unaffected. This directly targets the dominant additive build (Grinder, Doorman, Bookkeeper) while leaving multiplier and hype crew relatively intact — and it creates a floor-specific meta shift where multiplier builds suddenly outperform additive builds. Much more interesting than a blanket off-switch.

**Option B — Licensing Fee**
Each crew trigger costs a small bankroll fee (flat amount scaled to floor target). Grinder firing 15 times a shooter becomes an expensive choice. Players can disable individual crew themselves to avoid the cost. Creates per-crew cost-benefit decisions that are absent in the current version.

**Option C — Selective Licensing**
At the start of each marker, Mme. Le Prix announces two crew categories that are disabled for that marker (e.g., "TABLE and HYPE disabled"). Categories rotate each marker so players can't build around a single exemption. Now there are active decisions and mid-floor adaptation instead of a blanket off-switch.

**Option D — The Auction**
All crew start disabled. Each shooter, players bid bankroll to re-enable one crew member for that shooter only. Running a full crew is expensive; running none is free. Constant tension between spending bankroll to activate crew vs. conserving for bets.

### Verdict
**Change recommended.** Option A (The Tariff) is the priority pick. It's mechanically clean, requires minimal implementation (one multiplier applied to additives in settleTurn), and creates a genuine strategic shift within the floor. It also positions Mme. Le Prix as the boss who specifically counters additive-heavy strategies — a role the game currently lacks at F3. Option C (Selective Licensing) is the most interesting long-term but needs a category announcement UI. Recommend shipping The Tariff now and revisiting Selective Licensing later.

## Floor 4 — The Executive
**Rule:** FOURS_INSTANT_LOSS — any come-out roll of 4 ends the run immediately

### Assessment
Generates exciting moments but is fundamentally arbitrary. Rolling a 4 isn't a decision the player made — it's dice variance that kills a run. There's no skill expression, no way to engage with it strategically, and the instant-loss on the first 4 creates runs that end on roll 1 with no recourse. The Precision Counter build (Mechanic + Physics Prof + Oracle) can mitigate it, but only if those specific crew show up in the pub draft. The fun comes from tension; the arbitrariness comes from the lack of agency.

**Player feedback:** Fun but arbitrary.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — The Fine Print**
Three-strike system instead of instant loss. First 4: lose 20% of bankroll. Second 4: lose 40% of current bankroll. Third 4: run over. Eliminates dying to a single roll on turn 1 while keeping the threat real and escalating. Players feel the danger building and have two chances to adapt before the final consequence.

**Option B — The Clause**
A come-out 4 is still instant loss — unless the player has a hardway 4 bet live. If the hardway 4 is active when the 4 hits, the player survives and collects at standard hardway odds. Creates a genuine hedging mechanic: spend bankroll betting hardway 4 every come-out as insurance. That's a real strategic decision with a real cost. Adds skill expression to what's currently pure variance.

**Option C — Hostile Takeover**
A 4 doesn't kill the run. Instead, The Executive permanently acquires the player's highest-contributing crew member for the rest of the floor (returned after). First 4: lose your best crew. Second 4: lose second best. Third 4: game over. Racing to clear the marker before losing too many key pieces creates urgency without arbitrary death.

**Option D — Moving Target**
The dangerous number shifts each marker: 4 on marker 1, 10 on marker 2, 6 on marker 3. All three are come-out numbers that can set points. Rotating the dangerous number keeps the mechanic fresh within the floor and forces ongoing adaptation.

### Verdict
**Light change recommended.** Option A (The Fine Print) is the cleanest fix. It preserves the fun tension of "there's a number that will hurt you" while eliminating the arbitrary feel of single-roll death. Three-strike escalation is immediately legible and fair. Option B (The Clause) is the most interesting design because it adds genuine skill expression and hardway synergy — consider for a v2 iteration. Hostile Takeover (Option C) is worth noting as a future design thread but has more implementation complexity.

## Floor 5 — The Hierophant
**Rule:** TRIBUTE — 15% bankroll seizure on every 7-out (halved by THE_COVENANT comp)

### Assessment
Mechanically interesting concept — the mob takes a cut when your shooter dies — but it only bites when the player is already having a bad time. The Grinder/Doorman additive build, which generates income through NO_RESOLUTION rolls without caring about 7-out frequency, barely feels this boss. Players who are good at surviving long shooter runs barely trigger it. The tribute is real but it punishes the wrong thing.

**Player feedback:** Covered in F5-F9 combined analysis; identified as not effectively countering dominant strategy.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — Corrupt the Additives (Escrow)**
Additives don't pay out immediately — they're held in escrow. On 7-out, The Hierophant seizes 25% of the escrow before it settles. Suddenly Grinder is a direct liability: every NO_RESOLUTION additive is one seven-out away from partial confiscation. Forces players to actually protect their shooter instead of passively milking blank rolls. This is the most impactful change: it makes the dominant strategy specifically dangerous.

**Option B — Hype Tithe**
Tribute percentage scales with hype tier at time of seven-out. Below Heating Up: 10%. Heating Up: 20%. On Fire: 35%. Directly punishes the meta strategy of ramping hype to On Fire. The higher you've built, the more you stand to lose on a seven-out. Going On Fire becomes a calculated risk rather than a pure win state.

**Option C — Offering Escalation**
Tribute grows per marker cleared on the floor: 10% on marker 1, 15% on marker 2, 22% on marker 3. Forces urgency — clearing markers fast becomes a tactical priority. Rewards aggressive play while punishing a slow additive grind.

**Option D — The Debt**
A small running tribute debt accumulates each roll (flat amount). Seized in full on seven-out regardless of current bankroll. Makes every point-phase roll feel like a ticking meter. Can't escape by running short shooters — the debt still builds, just slower.

### Verdict
**Change recommended.** Option A (Escrow) is the strongest pick because it specifically targets the dominant additive strategy — the thing The Hierophant currently doesn't threaten. Option B (Hype Tithe) is the best secondary choice; it can stack with Option A for a two-pronged attack (your additives are at risk AND your hype investment is at risk). Together they make The Hierophant feel like an active threat rather than a passive tax on bad luck.

## Floor 6 — The Sovereign
**Rule:** TIDAL_SURGE — minimum bet floods 15% of marker target every 5 rolls for 2-roll surge windows

### Assessment
Mechanically distinct from Sarge's rising minimums — the surge cycle creates rhythmic pressure rather than steady escalation. Players learn to anticipate the cycle and time their bets around it. Thematically coherent (oceanic flood/tide metaphor, Atlantis floor). The 5-roll cadence gives players enough breathing room to plan without the boss feeling toothless.

**Player feedback:** Not specifically flagged as a problem.

### Verdict
**No changes recommended.** The Sovereign's tidal cycle is the most mechanically distinctive boss in the roster and creates a legitimate timing game. It's an appropriate difficulty step between The Hierophant (F5) and The Commander (F7). Monitor whether players learn to trivially time around the surge window — if so, consider adding a variance element (surge window length varies 1-3 rolls) in a future pass.

## Floor 7 — The Commander
**Rule:** ORBITAL_DECAY — hype multiplied by 0.5× on every 7-out; no floor (can fall below 1.0×)

### Assessment
"Hype decay on 7-out, but worse" — qualitatively identical to the hype reset every other boss causes, just quantitatively more punishing. The AA Meeting counter-build (Faith Healer + Warden + Lucky Charm) largely neutralizes it. Grinder/Doorman builds don't particularly care because their additive income doesn't require high hype to function. The boss doesn't feel like a Commander — it feels like a passive environmental hazard.

**Player feedback:** Effectively analyzed as The Captain — identified as not sufficiently distinct or threatening.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — High Altitude = High Risk**
Invert the threat entirely. The Commander doesn't punish *low* hype — he punishes *high* hype. At Heating Up (≥1.5×): 7-out triggers 60% decay instead of 50%. At On Fire (≥2.5×): 7-out triggers 80% decay AND a flat bankroll penalty. Getting to On Fire becomes dangerous. This directly counters the meta (build hype, ride On Fire forever) without touching additive builds that don't stack hype.

**Option B — The Death Spiral**
Hard consequence thresholds below 1.0×: below 0.75× a crew member is randomly suppressed next shooter; below 0.5× pass line max bet is halved; below 0.25× the shooter is forcibly ended. Current implementation lets hype go sub-1.0× with no special consequence beyond weak payouts. Adding a punishing cascade below 1.0× makes recovery feel urgent and creates a genuine "death spiral" that thematically fits The Commander.

**Option C — Scorched Earth**
On 7-out, hype decays as normal AND all pending additives from that shooter's last roll are wiped. Makes Grinder's point-phase income vulnerable to shooter transitions in a way ORBITAL_DECAY currently doesn't.

**Option D — Overextension Penalty**
After a shooter's 8th roll, each subsequent roll has an increasing chance of a "flanking maneuver" — a random negative effect (hype tick reversed, next additive stolen, crew ability misfires). Punishes long-shooter survival builds specifically. Short-lived shooters are safer than long ones — a completely inverted incentive from normal play.

### Verdict
**Change recommended.** Options A + B together. High Altitude = High Risk makes the dominant hype-stacking strategy specifically dangerous (punishes On Fire state rather than ignoring it), while The Death Spiral below 1.0× makes recovery feel genuinely urgent. Together they create a narrow safe zone — high enough to earn, low enough not to get obliterated — that requires active hype management rather than passive accumulation. This is thematically appropriate for a Commander who demands discipline.

## Floor 8 — The Emissary
**Rule:** FIRST_CONTACT_PROTOCOL — come-out 7/11 naturals nullified, replaced with a blank re-roll

### Assessment
The weakest boss in the roster. "No naturals" is nearly invisible by F8 because the dominant build (Grinder + Doorman) doesn't depend on come-out outcomes at all. The Regular is the only crew with a hard NATURAL dependency. Holly fires on POINT_HIT (unaffected). Grinder fires on NO_RESOLUTION (unaffected). Most players report the floor feeling like a non-event. An alien contact boss on the penultimate floor should feel dread-inducing, not shrug-worthy.

**Player feedback:** "Who cares?" — only matters for natural-based strategies, which are not the meta.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — Transmission Delay**
Additives don't pay out on the roll they're earned — they're held and paid on the *next* roll. If the shooter seven-outs before that next roll, pending additives evaporate. Directly guts Grinder: every NO_RESOLUTION additive is always one 7-out from vanishing. Creates constant existential anxiety about the shooter dying before their last additive settles.

**Option B — Static Interference**
Each crew member has a 20% chance per roll of misfiring — triggers, consumes any cooldown, generates zero effect. Probability-based, unblockable, constant low-grade anxiety. The more crew you're relying on (the dominant 5-slot full build), the more likely at least one fizzles every turn. Makes crew reliability feel corrupted.

**Option C — Frequency Blackout**
Every 4th point-phase roll is forced NO_RESOLUTION regardless of dice result. Even a 7-out that should end the shooter becomes a blank. Players see the 7 on the dice and watch it not resolve — deeply unsettling flavor for an alien-contact boss. Adds a frustrating floor of futility to otherwise good rolls.

**Option D — Crew Jamming**
One crew member is randomly "jammed" at the start of each new shooter (announced to the player). They can see who's jammed but can't change it. Forces strategic thinking: do you build with redundancy so a jammed slot doesn't cripple you? Changes crew construction philosophy for the floor.

### Verdict
**Change strongly recommended.** Option A (Transmission Delay) is the priority pick — it makes the dominant strategy (Grinder additive farming) specifically dangerous, requires minimal UI work (just holds and releases the additive value), and creates a tense decision every point-phase roll. Option B (Static Interference) is the most thematically resonant for an alien contact boss — the crew's communication channels are being jammed, hence the misfires — and could layer on top of Transmission Delay for a two-pronged experience. The Emissary is the most in need of a rethink of any boss in the roster.

## Floor 9 — The Architect
**Rule:** CONVERGENCE — one crew slot removed per 7-out (max 5); naked craps at count 5; no comp reward

### Assessment
The concept is strong — the floor slowly strips your tools away — but the execution has two problems. First, slots are removed sequentially (highest slot number first), which means the player's build doesn't meaningfully influence which crew survive. Second, by the time naked craps arrives, the run is usually already lost — so the endgame state doesn't generate real tension, just confirms defeat. The Architect should feel like a fight to hold your crew together; currently it feels like watching a slow countdown to oblivion.

**Player feedback:** Covered in F5-F9 combined analysis; identified as not targeting specific strategies.

### Redesign Ideas

**Option A — Targeted Demolition**
The Architect removes the *most recently triggered* crew member on 7-out, not the highest-numbered slot. Your most active crew die first. If Grinder fired this shooter, Grinder's slot is condemned. Creates a genuine dilemma: stop running your strongest crew to protect them, or keep firing and risk losing them. Forces players to think about cascade positioning and trigger frequency in a new way.

**Option B — Foundation Cracks**
Crew slots aren't removed outright — they degrade. Each 7-out applies a 20% effectiveness penalty to all crew (cumulative, multiplicative). After 4 seven-outs every crew fires at ~41% of base value. Players keep all 5 slots; they just become progressively useless. Less binary than disappearing, and creates a long slow death that's more agonizing than an abrupt removal.

**Option C — The Blueprint Revealed**
At floor entry, The Architect announces the condemned category order for the floor: e.g., "PAYOUT → HYPE → TABLE → DICE → WILDCARD." On each 7-out, all crew of the next condemned category are disabled (not removed). Players know the schedule in advance and must decide whether to restructure. Much more strategic than random slot loss — players can plan around the revealed order.

**Option D — Load-Bearing**
When a crew slot is removed, all remaining crew gain a cumulative "overload" penalty — additives reduced by 15% per removed slot (stacking). Going from 5 to 4 crew: everyone at 85%. 4 to 3: everyone at 72%. Makes each additional 7-out progressively more punishing even for crew that survive, and makes the final naked-craps threshold feel earned rather than arbitrary.

### Verdict
**Change recommended.** Option A (Targeted Demolition) is the priority pick — it creates the most interesting strategic tension by making your choices about which crew to run directly affect which crew survive. Option D (Load-Bearing) layers naturally on top: slots are removed by activity (Option A), and remaining crew take on increasing weight (Option D). Together they make The Architect feel like an active adversary dismantling your specific build, rather than a countdown timer.

## Final Recommendations

### Priority Tier — Ship These

| Boss | Change | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **The Emissary (F8)** | Transmission Delay — additives held one roll, evaporate on 7-out | Currently negligible. This makes the dominant strategy (Grinder) specifically dangerous with minimal implementation cost. Highest ROI change in the roster. |
| **Mme. Le Prix (F3)** | The Tariff — 35% additive reduction instead of blanket disable | Boring is worse than hard. The Tariff creates a floor-specific meta shift and keeps crew decisions live throughout the fight. Single multiplier in settleTurn. |
| **The Commander (F7)** | High Altitude = High Risk + Death Spiral below 1.0× | On Fire should be dangerous, not just powerful. Makes the dominant hype-stacking meta feel risky for the first time. |
| **The Hierophant (F5)** | Escrow seizure — additives held, % seized on 7-out | Only boss that directly counters additive farming. Currently the most widely used strategy faces no boss-specific pressure. |

### Secondary Tier — Worth Doing

| Boss | Change | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **The Architect (F9)** | Targeted Demolition + Load-Bearing | Makes slot loss strategic rather than sequential. Your crew choices affect which crew survive. |
| **Sarge (F2)** | Non-compliance fine + odds bets included | Small changes. Adds teeth without reworking the mechanic. |
| **The Executive (F4)** | The Fine Print (three-strike system) | Removes arbitrary single-roll death while keeping the threat. Fun stays, arbitrary goes. |

### Keep As-Is

| Boss | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **The Foreman (F1)** | Perfect intro boss. Legible, fair, thematically resonant. |
| **The Sovereign (F6)** | Most mechanically distinct boss in the game. Timing-based rhythm is genuinely unique. |

---

### The Core Problem This Solves

Right now, the dominant strategy (Grinder + Doorman additive build → ramp hype to On Fire → ride multipliers) faces no boss that specifically targets it. Every boss either punishes 7-outs (which additive builds don't rely on) or blocks a single trigger condition (which additive builds route around). 

The four Priority Tier changes collectively target additive farming from four different angles:
- **Hierophant:** Your additives are held hostage
- **Emissary:** Your additives evaporate if the shooter dies
- **Commander:** Your hype investment becomes a liability
- **Mme. Le Prix:** Your additive volume is taxed directly

A player running the dominant Grinder build will feel each of these bosses specifically, rather than breezing through them. Builds that don't rely on additives (multiplier builds, dice control builds, hype-only builds) will have a genuinely different experience on each floor — which is what "variety of battle" actually means.
